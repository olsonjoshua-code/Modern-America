You have navigated four decades of American history, from the Reagan Revolution to the digital age. Every era forced a choice: adapt to a changing world — or hold firm to enduring principles.
What was gained from embracing change? Economic growth, global connection, expanding rights. What was lost or left unresolved? Inequality, political division, the erosion of shared truth, the cost of endless war. The debates you encountered, security vs. freedom, globalization vs. workers, intervention vs. restraint, have no final answers. They are the permanent tensions of American democracy.
